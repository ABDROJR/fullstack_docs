Biryani Recipe

This spicy biryani has got to be one of the most flavourful biryani versions and is extremely popular all over the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg mutton/goat, cut into big pieces (or one chicken cut into 8 pieces)
  • 1/4 cup yoghurt
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 green chilli, chopped
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 5 cups long grain basmati rice

Method

  1. Marinate the meat in the remaining marinade ingredients for at least 2 hours or preferably refrigerate overnight.
  2. This helps tenderise the meat and builds flavour. Remember to remove the meat half an hour before you begin cooking to bring it to room temperature.
  3. Deep fry the potato chunks on low heat until a knife pierced through the centre of each potato piece yields easily
  4. Drain and set aside (you may opt to boil the potatoes if you like, but I find they hold their shape and taste a lot better fried).
  5. Wash and then soak the rice for about 20-30 minutes.
  6. Heat the half cup of oil in a deep pan and then fry the onion slices until golden.
  7. Remove a quarter of the fried onions (these will be used later) and leave the rest in the pan
  8. Add the whole spices (cumin, cadamom, pepper, cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon and black pepper).
  9. Once they sizzle, add the garlic and ginger pastes and stir them in until aromatic
  10. Next, add tomatoes, prunes, turmeric, salt and red chilli powder.
